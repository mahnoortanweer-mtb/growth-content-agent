# Skill: Internet Research & EdTech Trend Monitoring

## Purpose
Search the internet for EdTech trends, LinkedIn viral patterns, and competitive intelligence to supplement Rumi data. This ensures posts reference what's happening in the real world, not just internal metrics.

## Prerequisites
- Internet access for web searches
- Awareness of what's trending in EdTech (globally and in Pakistan)
- Understanding of verified vs. unverified claims
- Knowledge of Taleemabad's market positioning

## Instructions

### Step 1: Identify the Research Angle
When writing a post, ask: **"What's the broader context?"**
- Is this feature trend aligned with a global EdTech trend?
- Are there policy discussions happening around this topic?
- What are educators saying on LinkedIn about this?
- Are there competitive dynamics we should reference?

### Step 2: Search for Verified Insights
Look for real, cited insights (not inflated market size claims):
- **Policy gaps:** What regulations or governance challenges exist?
- **Practitioner pain points:** What are teachers, admins, students actually struggling with?
- **Behavioral patterns:** How are students/teachers actually using tools?
- **Market movements:** Who's investing, what's consolidating, what's emerging?

### Step 3: Cross-Reference with Rumi Data
Connect external trends to internal metrics:
- Not: "The EdTech market is growing" (generic)
- Yes: "The EdTech market is consolidating on profitability, and our retention curves show why..."

### Step 4: Build the Narrative
Use internet research to answer: **"Why should someone care about Rumi's data?"**

Example:
```
External trend: "Only 13% of districts have clear AI policies"
Rumi data: "Our AI coaching adoption is 45% in 2 weeks"
Post angle: "While 87% of districts lack AI governance, here's why we built 
differently — policy-first, adoption-second."
```

## Verified EdTech Insights (As of June 2026)

### **Taleemabad's Core Problem**
- **Reality:** 12+ million girls out of school in Pakistan
- **Opportunity:** Smartphone-based, low-cost remote learning can reach them
- **Post angle:** "We're not chasing growth metrics. We're solving an education crisis."

### **AI Governance Gap (Global)**
- **Reality:** Only 13% of school districts have clear AI policies
- **Implication:** 87% of districts are adopting AI tools without guidelines
- **Post angle:** "AI in education is growing fast, but most institutions lack policies. That's why we..."

### **Student Behavior with AI**
- **Reality:** Students show metacognitive laziness — they offload thinking instead of learning
- **Risk:** Widening achievement gaps (high-ability students use AI strategically; low-ability students skip effort)
- **Post angle:** "AI tools exist. But do they make students lazy or smarter? Here's what we're learning..."

### **Instructor Efficiency vs. Student Learning**
- **Reality:** AI tools improve teacher efficiency, but don't automatically improve student outcomes
- **Key insight:** Efficiency ≠ learning gains
- **Post angle:** "We obsess over student learning, not teacher convenience. Here's why..."

## How to Use This in Post Generation

When generating a post:
1. **Start with Rumi data** (internal truth)
2. **Layer in internet research** (external context)
3. **Connect the two** (relevance)
4. **End with perspective** (why Taleemabad is different)

### Example Flow

**Rumi finding:** "Lesson Plans adoption spiked 45% this week"

**Internet research:** "Teachers are drowning in administrative work (policy gap + practitioner pain point)"

**Connected post:**
```
Teachers asked for it. We listened.

Lesson Plans just shipped, and adoption is already 45% in first week — 
but here's what surprised us: teachers aren't just adopting it, they're 
asking for more planning tools.

Why? Because 70% of teacher time is admin, not teaching. While the 
EdTech world obsesses over engagement metrics, teachers need friction reduction.

That's a different game entirely.

#EdTech #Teachers
```

## Common Mistakes & How to Avoid

| Mistake | Avoidance |
|---------|-----------|
| Citing inflated market numbers | **Only use claims that have 2+ independent sources.** Market size claims are often unverified. |
| Generic trend statements | **Cite specific practitioner insights, not industry reports.** "Teachers are struggling with X" beats "EdTech is growing." |
| Disconnecting internet research from Rumi data | **Always connect them.** Internet research is context; Rumi data is proof. |
| Following competitors instead of users | **Listen to educators, not competing companies.** What do teachers actually say on LinkedIn? |
| Using outdated research | **Refresh internet research weekly.** EdTech moves fast. Last month's trend is old news. |

## Tools & Resources

**For monitoring:**
- LinkedIn (search education, AI, EdTech hashtags)
- Twitter/X (search #EdTech, #Education, #AI)
- Google Scholar (academic research on learning outcomes)
- Product Hunt (new EdTech tools, what's gaining traction)
- EdTech publications (EdWeek, Learning Policy Institute, UNESCO reports)

**For verification:**
- MIT's Solve (real-world education problems, vetted solutions)
- UNESCO Institute (global education data, policy)
- Gallup (educator surveys)
- Academic papers (ResearchGate, arXiv for behavioral studies)

**For Pakistan-specific:**
- Pakistan Bureau of Statistics (education data)
- SAHE (South Asian education research)
- Local news sources (teacher sentiment, policy changes)

## Notes
- **Update weekly:** EdTech moves fast. Refresh `docs/internet-insights.md` every week
- **Verify before citing:** One source is a claim; two sources is a pattern
- **Connect to Rumi:** Internet research without internal data feels generic
- **User voice > market data:** What educators say matters more than what analysts predict
- **Document sources:** Always cite. LinkedIn followers trust transparency over hype
