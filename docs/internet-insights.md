## **Global EdTech Trends (Verified)**

### **AI in Schools: The Governance Gap**
**The Trend:**
- Only 13% of school districts have clear AI policies communicated to students and teachers (EdWeek Research)
- 87% of schools are adopting AI tools WITHOUT policies, guidance, or governance

**Why It Matters:**
- Schools are moving fast but flying blind
- There's a huge opportunity for "responsible AI in education" positioning

**Post Angle:**
- "While 87% of districts lack AI policies, here's how we're building differently"
- "AI adoption is outpacing governance. Schools need frameworks, not just tools."
- "Policy-first, then adoption. Here's why we started there."

### **Student Behavior with AI: The Laziness Problem**
**The Trend:**
- Students show "metacognitive laziness" when using AI chatbots (arxiv peer-reviewed research)
- They engage in "cognitive offloading" — delegating thinking instead of learning
- Achievement gaps are widening: high-ability students use AI strategically; low-ability students skip effort

**Why It Matters:**
- AI tools CAN make students lazier, not smarter
- Just "adding AI" to education doesn't guarantee learning gains

**Post Angle:**
- "We use AI. But does it make students lazy or smarter? Here's what we measure."
- "High-ability students strategize with AI. Low-ability students check out. We're building to close that gap."
- "AI tools exist. The question is: are they learning multipliers or thinking shortcuts?"

### **Instructor Efficiency ≠ Student Learning**
**The Trend:**
- AI tools improve teacher efficiency (grading automation, scheduling, lesson planning)
- BUT: Student learning outcomes show NO statistically significant improvement in most cases
- The disconnect: Teachers save time, but students don't learn better

**Why It Matters:**
- Efficiency tools are not teaching tools
- Growth teams obsess over adoption; teachers care about learning

**Post Angle:**
- "We obsess over student learning outcomes, not teacher convenience metrics"
- "Automation feels good. But does it teach? Here's what our data shows."
- "Every feature gets asked: Does this help students learn better? Not just faster."

## **EdTech Market Dynamics**

### **What's NOT Verified (Debunked Claims)**
These numbers are circulating online but NOT well-sourced:
- ❌ "EdTech market will reach $380B-$600B by 2026-2032"
- ❌ "86% of organizations have adopted generative AI"
- ❌ "EdTech platforms show 70% dropout reduction"
- ❌ Market size projections ($7.57B → $112B)

**Why they don't work for LinkedIn posts:**
- Unverified claims look like marketing hype
- Educators distrust inflated numbers
- Competitors use the same ones

### **What IS Happening (Real Market Signals)**
- **Consolidation:** Market is consolidating around profitability, not growth
- **Policy lag:** Regulation is slow; adoption is fast (creates opportunity)
- **Verification gap:** Schools want proof of learning outcomes, not just adoption rates
- **Teacher voice rising:** Educator feedback is increasingly central to product decisions

## **LinkedIn Viral Patterns (EdTech)**

### **What Gets Engagement**
1. **Specific numbers over vague claims:** "45% adoption in week 1" beats "rapid growth"
2. **Teacher stories over market data:** What one teacher accomplished > $1B market size claim
3. **Honest struggle over polished metrics:** "Here's what we got wrong" gets more comments than "Here's our success"
4. **Problem-first over solution-first:** Lead with "teachers are drowning in admin" not "our tool automates grading"
5. **Student impact over business metrics:** Focus on learning outcomes not DAU/MAU

### **What Doesn't Work**
- Generic market size claims
- Inflated adoption numbers
- Competitive shade (educators ignore it)
- "We're disruptive" positioning (educators eye-roll)
- Metrics without context
