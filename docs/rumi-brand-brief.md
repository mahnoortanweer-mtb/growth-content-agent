# Brand Brief: Rumi

**Source:** Official brand brief document
**Website:** https://hellorumi.ai/
**Status:** Branding in progress — visual identity (colors, fonts, logo) being finalized by agency

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Rumi is an AI-powered educational support tool that lives entirely on WhatsApp. Designed to assist teachers by automating high-friction tasks: lesson planning, coaching, and resource creation.

Currently expanding globally — pilots and partnerships active or launching across 5 countries: Sri Lanka, Kenya, Tanzania, Yemen, and Pakistan.

**Goal:** Move Rumi from a "functional bot" to a recognizable, trusted global education brand that originates from Pakistan.

---

## 2. Core Identity

- **The "Saathi" (Companion):** Supportive, approachable, reliable companion for educators
- **The Greeting:** "Hello Rumi." — the primary brand hook. A simple greeting that starts a helpful conversation.
- **The Philosophy:** "AI-augmented humanity." We don't replace the teacher — we give them tools (voice-to-text, instant planning, coaching) so they can focus on students, not paperwork.

---

## 3. Strategic Positioning

- **Native to WhatsApp:** The brand must feel like a contact in your phonebook, not external software
- **"Brand Pakistan" Innovation:** Rumi is a flagship for Pakistani innovation — world-class EdTech built here, exported globally
- **Frictionless Utility:** "If you can send a voice note, you can use Rumi."

---

## 4. Visual Direction

### Aesthetic
- **Minimalist & Clean:** No "high-tech" clutter
- **Two-tone color palette:** Legible on low-resolution mobile screens
- **Human-Centric:** Celebrate the teacher's work — Rumi is the invisible engine behind their success

### Tone of Voice
- **Bilingual & Forgiving:** Accessible to native and non-native English speakers (Urdu, Swahili, etc.)
- **Encouraging:** Helpful peer, not a rigid authority figure — makes your day easier

### What This Means for LinkedIn Images
- Keep it simple — two colors max per image
- Warm, human feel — not cold or corporate
- High contrast text (readable on mobile screens)
- Teacher/classroom context over abstract tech imagery
- Short, clear text — no clutter

## 6. Agency Scope (for context)

**Phase 1 — Brand Foundations:**
- WhatsApp-optimized logo and icon set
- Global messaging framework ("Hello Rumi" across cultural contexts)

**Phase 2 — Collateral & Content:**
- Infographics and short-form video templates ("Tech News for Teachers")
- User onboarding: designing the "First Chat" experience

**Phase 3 — Institutional Positioning:**
- Partner decks for educational authorities (TEDA) and international NGOs

---

## 7. Key Brand Challenges

1. **Simple vs. Sophisticated:** Accessible to low-tech teachers while looking world-class to global partners
2. **Platform Constraints:** Maintaining a distinct Rumi identity inside WhatsApp's UI
3. **Cultural Neutrality:** Visual language equally effective in East Africa and South Asia

---

## How to Use This in Post Generation

When writing posts attributed to any team member about Rumi:
- Lead with the teacher, not the technology
- Frame Rumi as a "saathi" — always present, never the hero
- Use simple, accessible language (avoid jargon)
- Global ambition + Pakistani origin = a story worth telling
- "Hello Rumi" is a strong hook for posts about new features or onboarding
